Pittsburgh Real Estate FAQ

Common questions about buying and selling in Allegheny County, Washington County, and Pittsburgh suburbs

We serve all of Allegheny County — including Pittsburgh city neighborhoods (Shadyside, Lawrenceville, Squirrel Hill, Strip District, Southside) and suburbs (Mt. Lebanon, Bethel Park, Upper St. Clair, North Hills, Monroeville, Penn Hills, Fox Chapel). We also serve Washington County including Canonsburg, Washington city, and Charleroi, plus parts of Westmoreland, Butler, and Beaver counties.

Allegheny County home prices range from roughly $165,000 to $425,000 with a county median near $220,000. Prices vary widely by community: luxury suburbs like Upper St. Clair ($420K–$750K+) and Mt. Lebanon ($380K–$650K) command premium prices for top school districts and estates. Mid-range suburbs like Bethel Park ($310K–$520K) and North Hills ($220K–$380K) offer family-friendly value. More affordable communities like Monroeville ($160K–$280K) and Penn Hills ($185K–$320K) offer strong entry points.

Through our partnership with Three Rivers Lending, we can get you pre-approved in under 12 hours — making you a serious buyer in any Allegheny County or Washington County market. This speed gives you a competitive edge, especially in fast-moving suburbs like North Hills where homes average just 24 days on market.

For families, the top Pittsburgh suburbs depend on budget and priorities. Top schools + luxury: Upper St. Clair and Mt. Lebanon. Strong schools + value: Bethel Park ($310K–$520K) with its highly regarded school district and family community. New construction + growth: North Hills with modern homes and fastest appreciation in the metro (3.6%). Budget-friendly family living: Washington County communities like Canonsburg offer quality family neighborhoods from $125K–$280K.

Yes — Washington County is an excellent emerging investment market. Homes average $130K–$350K (well below Allegheny County), with 2.5% annual appreciation and growing population. Key investment opportunities exist in Canonsburg's growing community, Washington city's downtown revitalization, and Charleroi's riverfront area. Its proximity to Pittsburgh via I-79 and Route 19 ensures ongoing demand from buyers priced out of Allegheny County.

Our "perfect storm" strategy combines: (1) Strategic pricing that attracts multiple buyers without leaving money on the table, (2) Aggressive digital and social media marketing tailored to your specific Allegheny County or Washington County neighborhood, (3) Our strong industry network generating pre-qualified buyer interest before listing day, and (4) Professional presentation that maximizes perceived value. This approach has generated multiple competing offers across Pittsburgh neighborhoods and suburbs at all price points.
