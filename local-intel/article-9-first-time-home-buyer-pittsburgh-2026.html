First-Time Home Buyer in Pittsburgh: Programs, Grants, and Down Payment Help (2026)

Updated April 2026  |  By the We Sell Any Home Team

First-time home buyers in Pittsburgh have access to some of the strongest state assistance programs in the country, including forgivable grants that cover your entire down payment. The Pennsylvania Housing Finance Agency (PHFA) offers multiple loan and assistance programs specifically designed for first-time buyers, and Pittsburgh's median home price of approximately $233,000 (Redfin, February 2026) makes this one of the most affordable major metro areas in the U.S. to purchase your first home.

This guide breaks down every major PHFA program available to Pittsburgh buyers in 2026, explains how much you actually need for a down payment, and identifies which neighborhoods are most accessible for buyers entering the market for the first time.

How Much Down Payment Do You Actually Need?

The down payment requirement depends entirely on your loan type. Here is what each loan requires on a $250,000 Pittsburgh home:

Loan Type Minimum Down Amount on $250K Home
Conventional 3% to 20% $7,500 to $50,000
FHA 3.5% $8,750
VA (veterans only) 0% $0
USDA (rural eligible areas) 0% $0

Even if you choose a conventional or FHA loan, Pennsylvania offers assistance programs that can cover most or all of your down payment. Below are the specific PHFA programs available to Pittsburgh buyers in 2026.

PHFA Program 1: Keystone Home Loan

The Keystone Home Loan is PHFA's flagship mortgage product for first-time home buyers in Pennsylvania. It offers a 30-year fixed-rate mortgage and supports FHA, VA, and conventional loan types. The fixed rate means your monthly payment stays the same for the life of the loan, which provides predictability that adjustable-rate mortgages do not.

The Keystone Home Loan is designed specifically for low-to-moderate income buyers and carries income limits that vary by county and household size. Buyers must occupy the home as their primary residence and complete any required homebuyer education.

PHFA Program 2: HFA Preferred Loan

The HFA Preferred loan is another 30-year fixed-rate option through PHFA, designed for low-to-moderate income buyers who want a low down payment conventional loan. This program is particularly attractive because it can be paired with the $500 PHFA Grant, which provides $500 toward your down payment at no cost to you.

Both the Keystone Home Loan and HFA Preferred require borrowers with credit scores below 680 to complete in-person homebuyer counseling. This counseling covers budgeting, the mortgage process, and homeownership responsibilities. Buyers with scores of 680 or above may be exempt from this requirement.

PHFA Program 3: Keystone Advantage Assistance

Keystone Advantage Assistance is a down payment and closing cost assistance program structured as a 0% interest second mortgage. Eligible buyers can receive up to 4% of the purchase price or a maximum of $6,000, whichever is less. This second mortgage is repaid over 10 years with no interest charged.

On a $233,000 home (Pittsburgh's current median), this program would provide up to $6,000 toward your down payment and closing costs at 0% interest. That covers most or all of the minimum down payment required for an FHA loan on a home at that price point.

Keystone Advantage Quick Facts

Up to 4% of purchase price (max $6,000). 0% interest. Repaid over 10 years. Can be combined with Keystone Home Loan or HFA Preferred.

PHFA Program 4: K-FIT (Keystone Forgivable in Ten Years)

K-FIT is the most generous PHFA assistance program available to Pittsburgh buyers. It provides up to 5% of the purchase price with no maximum dollar limit. Unlike Keystone Advantage, K-FIT is a forgivable second mortgage, meaning you do not have to repay it as long as you remain in the home and meet program requirements over the 10-year forgiveness period.

On a $233,000 home, K-FIT would provide up to $11,650 in down payment assistance that is fully forgiven over 10 years. On a $300,000 home, the assistance would be up to $15,000. There are no monthly payments required on the K-FIT assistance during the forgiveness period.

K-FIT is available to buyers using either the Keystone Home Loan or HFA Preferred mortgage. Funding availability varies, so buyers should apply early and work with a PHFA-approved lender.

PHFA $500 Grant

Borrowers using the HFA Preferred loan are eligible for an additional $500 PHFA Grant toward their down payment. This is a true grant with no repayment required. While $500 alone will not cover a full down payment, it stacks with other assistance programs and reduces the total cash a buyer needs at closing.

How to Apply for PHFA Programs

PHFA programs are not applied for directly through the state. Instead, you work with a PHFA-approved lender who processes your mortgage application and determines your eligibility for each assistance program. The general steps are:

  • ■Step 1: Check your credit score. Buyers with scores below 680 should complete in-person homebuyer counseling before applying.
  • ■Step 2: Find a PHFA-approved lender in the Pittsburgh area. Your real estate agent can recommend one.
  • ■Step 3: Get pre-approved with the lender, specifying you want to use PHFA programs.
  • ■Step 4: The lender will determine which combination of programs (Keystone Home Loan or HFA Preferred, plus Keystone Advantage or K-FIT) gives you the best terms.
  • ■Step 5: Begin your home search with a clear understanding of your budget and assistance amount.

Most Accessible Pittsburgh Neighborhoods for First-Time Buyers

With a median home price around $233,000, Pittsburgh offers first-time buyers access to neighborhoods that would be out of reach in most other major cities. Several areas stand out for their combination of affordability, quality of life, and appreciation potential:

  • ■Dormont: Walkable community with trolley access to downtown. Homes frequently list below the metro median, and the neighborhood has a strong sense of community.
  • ■Brentwood: Affordable single-family homes with low crime rates and easy access to the South Hills. A strong option for buyers who want suburban feel without suburban prices.
  • ■Brookline: City of Pittsburgh neighborhood with a thriving small-business corridor on Brookline Boulevard. Prices remain accessible for first-time buyers.
  • ■Crafton: Small borough with a walkable downtown, parks, and homes that frequently fall within PHFA income and price limits.
  • ■Washington County suburbs: Communities like Canonsburg and Houston offer significantly lower home prices and property taxes compared to Allegheny County, while remaining within a 30-minute commute of downtown Pittsburgh.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum down payment for a first-time home buyer in Pittsburgh?

The minimum down payment depends on loan type. FHA loans require 3.5% down, which equals $8,750 on a $250,000 home. Conventional loans start at 3% down. PHFA programs like Keystone Advantage Assistance and K-FIT can cover most or all of your down payment with 0% interest loans or forgivable grants.

What PHFA programs are available for first-time buyers in Pennsylvania?

PHFA offers several programs: the HFA Preferred loan (30-year fixed, low down payment), Keystone Home Loan (30-year fixed for first-time buyers supporting FHA, VA, and conventional), Keystone Advantage Assistance (0% interest second mortgage up to 4% of purchase price, max $6,000), K-FIT (up to 5% of purchase price, fully forgiven over 10 years), and a $500 PHFA Grant for HFA Preferred borrowers.

Do I need homebuyer counseling to qualify for PHFA programs?

If your credit score is below 680, PHFA requires in-person homebuyer counseling before you can qualify for their loan programs. Buyers with credit scores of 680 or above may not need counseling, though completing it is always recommended.

What is the median home price in Pittsburgh in 2026?

The median home sale price in the Pittsburgh metro area is approximately $233,000 as of February 2026, according to Redfin data. This makes Pittsburgh significantly more affordable than most major metro areas in the United States.

Is the K-FIT down payment assistance really free money?

K-FIT provides up to 5% of the purchase price with no maximum dollar limit. The assistance is fully forgiven over 10 years as long as you remain in the home and meet program requirements. It functions as a forgivable second mortgage with no monthly payments required during the forgiveness period.
