What are homes selling for in Washington County PA?
Homes in Washington County typically range from $130,000 to $350,000 depending on the community, with areas like Canonsburg, McMurray, and Peters Township seeing strong buyer demand. Washington County remains one of the most affordable options compared to Allegheny County while still offering excellent schools and easy commutes to Pittsburgh. It's a growing market that WeSellAnyHome knows inside and out.
Is Cranberry Township a good place to buy a home?
Cranberry Township in Butler County is one of the fastest-growing communities in Pennsylvania, known for top-rated Seneca Valley schools, abundant new construction, and a thriving retail and dining scene. Homes typically range from $180,000 to $450,000. Its location along I-79 and the Pennsylvania Turnpike makes it an easy commute to downtown Pittsburgh, making it ideal for families and professionals alike.
