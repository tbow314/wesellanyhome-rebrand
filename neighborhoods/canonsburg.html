Canonsburg Overview

Canonsburg is a borough in Washington County, Pennsylvania, with a population of approximately 9,000 residents. Situated just 15 minutes south of Pittsburgh along the I-79 corridor, Canonsburg combines genuine small-town character with direct access to one of the region's largest employment hubs at Southpointe. The borough's tree-lined streets, walkable downtown, and tight-knit community make it one of the most undervalued real estate markets in the greater Pittsburgh area.

Founded in 1791, Canonsburg carries deep roots. It is famously the hometown of Perry Como, the legendary singer whose legacy lives on through a statue on Pike Street. The borough hosts one of the largest Fourth of July celebrations in western Pennsylvania, drawing thousands of visitors each year. That sense of community pride translates directly into well-maintained homes, active neighborhood associations, and a housing market that has consistently appreciated while remaining accessible to first-time buyers and young families.

The surrounding area — including Cecil Township, North Strabane Township, and the Southpointe business district — has experienced significant growth driven by the energy industry and technology sector. This economic engine supports Canonsburg's real estate market and provides residents with employment opportunities minutes from home, eliminating the need for long commutes into Pittsburgh.

Market Snapshot

Canonsburg's real estate market offers strong value relative to Pittsburgh's inner-ring suburbs. Home prices have risen steadily, driven by Southpointe expansion, limited inventory, and growing demand from professionals seeking affordability without sacrificing location. The market is competitive but accessible — a combination that makes Canonsburg one of the strongest buying opportunities in Washington County.

Neighborhoods & Surrounding Areas

Downtown Canonsburg & Pike Street Corridor

The heart of Canonsburg runs along Pike Street, where you will find local restaurants, shops, the Perry Como statue, and a walkable Main Street atmosphere. Housing stock in the borough center includes well-maintained Craftsman and Colonial homes, many dating to the early 1900s with updated interiors. Prices here are among the most affordable in the region, making downtown Canonsburg ideal for first-time buyers and investors.

Streets like West College, North Central Avenue, and East Pike offer a mix of single-family homes and townhouses with character. Proximity to Town Park, the Canonsburg Library, and local dining makes this area walkable and convenient.

Cecil Township & Southpointe

Adjacent to Canonsburg, Cecil Township has seen the most new construction in the area. The Southpointe business district — a 600-acre master-planned development — anchors the local economy with major employers including CONSOL Energy, Ansys, Range Resources, and dozens of energy and technology firms. Southpointe also features a golf club, hotels, and corporate dining.

Newer subdivisions in Cecil Township offer modern 3-4 bedroom homes in the $300K-$400K range, popular with professionals who want a short commute to Southpointe offices. The area shares the Canon-McMillan School District with Canonsburg proper.

North Strabane Township

Directly north of Canonsburg along Route 19, North Strabane Township offers retail convenience and residential variety. The Washington Crown Center and surrounding commercial corridor provide shopping and services. Housing ranges from starter homes to newer developments, all within the Canon-McMillan school boundary.

Houston & Chartiers Township

Just south of Canonsburg, Houston borough and Chartiers Township offer rural-suburban living with larger lot sizes and lower price points. These areas appeal to buyers seeking more land and privacy while staying within commuting distance of Southpointe and Pittsburgh. Homes here often sit on half-acre to one-acre lots.

Canon-McMillan School District

District Overview

The Canon-McMillan School District serves approximately 4,800 students across 8 schools. The district covers Canonsburg, Cecil Township, North Strabane Township, and surrounding communities. Canon-McMillan offers a comprehensive curriculum, competitive athletics program, and access to career-technical education through the Western Area Career & Technology Center.

The district has invested in facility upgrades and technology integration, keeping schools modern and competitive. Class sizes are manageable, and the district benefits from strong community support and engaged parent organizations.

Key Schools

  • Canon-McMillan High School — Grades 9-12. Strong college placement rates, AP course offerings, competitive WPIAL athletics. The Big Macs are a source of community pride.
  • Canon-McMillan Middle School — Grades 6-8. STEM programming and extracurricular activities.
  • Wylandville Elementary — One of several elementary schools serving the district with solid fundamentals and community engagement.
  • Cecil Elementary — Serves the growing Cecil Township population with modern facilities.

Lifestyle & Things to Do

Fourth of July Celebration

Canonsburg hosts one of the largest and longest-running Fourth of July celebrations in western Pennsylvania. The multi-day event features a parade, live music, carnival rides, food vendors, and a fireworks display that draws visitors from across the region. It is the defining community event of the year and a point of genuine pride for residents.

Perry Como Legacy

Canonsburg is the birthplace of Perry Como, one of the best-selling music artists of the 20th century. A bronze statue of Como stands on Pike Street, and the town celebrates his legacy through local events and the Perry Como Music Fest. The connection to Como gives Canonsburg a cultural identity that sets it apart from other suburban communities.

Dining & Local Businesses

Pike Street and the surrounding area offer a solid mix of local restaurants, pizza shops, and family-owned businesses. The Southpointe corridor adds upscale dining and national chains. Canonsburg's Italian heritage shows up in the food — local pizza and Italian restaurants are a cut above. The Sarris Candies factory in nearby Canonsburg is a regional destination for chocolate and ice cream.

Parks & Recreation

Canonsburg Town Park provides sports fields, playgrounds, and event space in the heart of the borough. The surrounding area offers access to Chartiers Creek for fishing, Mingo Creek County Park for hiking and mountain biking, and the Southpointe Golf Club for golfers. Peters Lake Park and the Montour Trail are within a short drive for additional outdoor recreation.

Commute & Transportation

Downtown Pittsburgh

25 minutes via I-79 North to I-279. Direct interstate access with no surface street bottlenecks. Reverse commuters from Pittsburgh to Southpointe enjoy an easy drive against traffic.

Southpointe Business District

5-10 minutes. Many Canonsburg residents walk or drive a short distance to Southpointe offices. This proximity is the single biggest driver of local housing demand.

Pittsburgh International Airport

30-35 minutes via I-79 North to the airport. Convenient for professionals who travel regularly. Faster access than many Pittsburgh neighborhoods.

I-79 is the primary transportation artery, running north-south through the area and connecting Canonsburg to Pittsburgh, Morgantown (WV), and Erie. Route 19 provides a secondary north-south corridor with commercial access. Public transit options are limited — most residents rely on personal vehicles.

Canonsburg is the hometown of legendary singer Perry Como and is known for hosting one of the largest Fourth of July celebrations in the Pittsburgh region. The borough has a charming downtown along Pike Street, strong community identity, and proximity to the Southpointe business and technology district.

Median prices in the $200K-$280K range are accessible with standard financing, the Canon-McMillan School District serves growing families well, and the Southpointe business district provides employment opportunities within minutes.

What is the Southpointe business district near Canonsburg?

Is the Canonsburg real estate market appreciating?
