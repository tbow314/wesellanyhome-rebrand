South Fayette Township Overview

South Fayette Township is a fast-growing township in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, with a population of approximately 16,000 residents and rising. Located 17 miles southwest of downtown Pittsburgh and just 15 minutes from Pittsburgh International Airport, South Fayette has become one of the most actively developed residential corridors in the region. The township pairs top-tier public schools with a steady pipeline of new construction, mixed-use development, and commercial expansion that has reshaped the local economy over the past decade.

The South Fayette Township School District is the headline. It holds an A+ rating from Niche, ranks in the top 5% of Pennsylvania districts on standardized test proficiency, and continues to attract relocating families from across the metro and out of state. That demand shows up directly in the housing market — well-priced listings inside the district boundary move quickly, often within the first ten days, and home values across the township have appreciated steadily year over year.

Beyond the schools, South Fayette is home to UPMC Children's South, Topgolf, and the Newbury Market redevelopment — a mixed-use master plan that includes a Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World opening in 2026, the Wembley Club pickleball and fitness facility, the Lafayette 180 shopping center, and Apex Newbury residential. Builders invested $75 million in South Fayette construction in 2024, a 57% increase over the prior year. The combination of top schools, modern construction, and a maturing commercial base makes South Fayette one of the most defensible long-term real estate markets in southwestern Pennsylvania.

Market Snapshot

Neighborhoods & Surrounding Areas

Newbury & The Newbury Market Corridor

The Newbury master plan is the centerpiece of South Fayette growth. The mixed-use development combines residential, retail, dining, and entertainment around the intersection of Route 50 and Newbury. A 99,000-square-foot Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World opens in 2026, joined by the Wembley Club pickleball facility, Lafayette 180 retail center, and Apex Newbury residential. Homes in and adjacent to Newbury trend newer and command the township's highest prices.

Buyers drawn to Newbury are typically families looking for new construction, walkable retail, and the strongest direct exposure to the township's commercial growth story.

Hastings & Battle Ridge

Established subdivisions in the Hastings and Battle Ridge sections of South Fayette offer 3-5 bedroom homes built primarily in the 2000s and 2010s. These communities sit firmly within the South Fayette Township School District boundary and offer modern floor plans, finished basements, and quarter-acre to half-acre lots. Pricing typically runs in the $450K-$650K range.

These neighborhoods are popular with families relocating into the district who want established landscaping, mature trees, and a built-out community feel rather than active construction zones.

Hickory Heights & Millers Run

Older sections of South Fayette near the Hickory Heights and Millers Run areas offer more affordable entry points, often in the high $300Ks to low $400Ks. Housing here ranges from ranches and capes from the 1970s and 1980s to mid-2000s subdivisions. These areas appeal to first-time buyers and downsizers who want district access without new-construction pricing.

McDonald Borough & Surrounding Areas

South Fayette shares the 15057 ZIP code with McDonald Borough and other small communities. McDonald itself is a separate municipality with a different school district, so confirming the address is critical. The broader area also includes parts of Cecil Township, North Fayette, and Collier Township, each with its own school assignment and price profile.

South Fayette Township School District

District Overview

The South Fayette Township School District holds an A+ rating from Niche, ranks #11 out of 678 Pennsylvania school districts based on math and reading proficiency, and is consistently named one of the top public school systems in the state. Math proficiency averages 74% — nearly double the Pennsylvania public school average of 38% — and reading proficiency averages 87% versus the 55% statewide average.

The district serves approximately 3,500 students across four schools and continues to expand facilities in response to enrollment growth. Strong academic outcomes, competitive athletics, and active parent organizations make it the primary draw for families relocating into the area.

Key Schools

  • South Fayette High School — Grades 9-12. Lions athletics, strong AP and honors program, top WPIAL athletic finishes, and high college placement rates.
  • South Fayette Middle School — Grades 6-8. STEAM-focused curriculum and active extracurriculars feeding into the high school.
  • South Fayette Intermediate School — Grades 3-5. Modern facility serving the upper-elementary population.
  • South Fayette Elementary School — Grades K-2. Foundation years for the district's growing student body.

Lifestyle & Things to Do

Newbury Market Development

Newbury Market anchors the township's lifestyle and commercial story. The mixed-use redevelopment includes the upcoming 99,000-square-foot Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World — the first in western Pennsylvania — alongside the Wembley Club pickleball facility, Lafayette 180 shopping center, and Apex Newbury residential. The development is reshaping how residents shop, dine, and spend weekends within the township itself.

Topgolf & UPMC Children's South

South Fayette is home to a Topgolf entertainment venue and UPMC Children's South, the regional pediatric outpatient and surgical center. Topgolf draws weekend traffic from across the metro, while UPMC Children's South gives families fast access to top-tier pediatric specialty care without driving to the main Lawrenceville campus.

Parks & Recreation

Fairview Park and Boys Home Run Park anchor the township's recreational footprint with sports fields, walking trails, playgrounds, and event space. The Panhandle Trail offers dozens of miles of rail-trail biking and walking through the region. Several private golf clubs and country clubs sit within a short drive, including Fox Chapel-area courses and the Bishop's Ridge area to the north.

Dining & Local Businesses

Dining options span national chains in the developing Newbury and Bridgeville commercial corridors and locally owned restaurants throughout the township. Adjacent communities including Bridgeville, Upper St. Clair, and Mt. Lebanon expand the dining radius significantly, all reachable within 10 to 15 minutes.

Commute & Transportation

Downtown Pittsburgh

15-25 minutes via I-79 North to I-376 East. Direct interstate access with a straightforward commute that runs faster than many closer-in suburbs depending on rush-hour conditions.

Pittsburgh International Airport

15 minutes via I-79 North to I-376 West. One of the most convenient airport-area submarkets, ideal for travelers, airline professionals, and Moon-area corporate commuters.

Southpointe & Cecil Corridor

15-20 minutes via I-79 South. Energy-sector and technology professionals at Southpointe can reach the office in roughly the same time as a standard cross-town Pittsburgh commute.

I-79 is the primary transportation artery, running north-south through the area and connecting South Fayette to Pittsburgh, the airport, and the Southpointe corridor. Route 50 provides east-west access through the township and links directly to the Newbury Market commercial core. Public transit options are limited — most residents rely on personal vehicles.

Who Should Buy in South Fayette Township

Families Relocating for Schools

If you are moving into the Pittsburgh metro and the school district is your top criterion, South Fayette belongs at the top of the shortlist. The district ranks in the top 5% statewide and consistently delivers the academic outcomes that justify the price-per-square-foot premium.

Airport-Area Professionals

If you work in Moon Township, at the airport, or anywhere along the I-376 corporate corridor, South Fayette puts you 15 minutes from the office while keeping your kids in a top-ranked school district. Few markets in the region offer that combination.

New Construction Buyers

South Fayette has one of the most active new-construction pipelines in Allegheny County. If you want a modern floor plan, energy-efficient build, finished basement, and the option to customize finishes, the active subdivisions in and around Newbury are the strongest fit.

Buying Guide for South Fayette Township

Buying in South Fayette Township rewards preparation and speed. The combination of top-ranked schools, active construction, and limited resale inventory means well-priced homes inside the district boundary move quickly — average days on market is just 19, and the sale-to-list ratio runs at 99% or higher. Get pre-approved and ready to make offers the same week a property hits the market. Confirm the school district boundary before falling in love with a listing — the township shares the 15057 ZIP code and surrounding areas with several other municipalities, and a quarter mile in the wrong direction can put a home into Chartiers Valley, Canon-McMillan, or McDonald school districts instead of South Fayette. New construction in Newbury and the surrounding subdivisions commands a premium per square foot but delivers modern floor plans, energy efficiency, and builder warranties. Established subdivisions like Hastings and Battle Ridge offer mature landscaping and slightly lower price points. Older homes in the Hickory Heights and Millers Run areas provide entry points into the high $300Ks. Compare lot size, basement condition, finished square footage, and proximity to Route 50 and I-79 access. With strong appreciation, continued commercial expansion at Newbury Market, and steady school-driven demand, buying now positions you ahead of continued growth in the corridor.

Selling Guide for South Fayette Township

Selling in South Fayette Township means leading with the school district and the Newbury growth story. Buyers in this market are overwhelmingly families relocating for the South Fayette Township School District and professionals drawn to the airport corridor — your listing copy, photography, and showings should reinforce both. Confirm the school assignment for the address with the district directly and put it in the marketing materials. Professional photography of the kitchen, family room, primary suite, and any outdoor living space drives the strongest early traffic. Price accurately using recent comps within the same school boundary — the 99% sale-to-list ratio means buyers expect fair pricing and will skip overpriced listings. Highlight Newbury Market access, commute times to Pittsburgh and the airport, and any recent updates to the kitchen, bathrooms, or mechanical systems. Spring and early summer align with the school-decision calendar and produce the strongest seasonal demand — list before April for peak buyer activity. Quality listings priced correctly typically receive multiple offers within the first ten days, and the South Fayette market continues to favor well-prepared sellers.

Frequently Asked Questions About South Fayette Township Real Estate

What is the average home price in South Fayette Township, PA?

The median home price in South Fayette Township ranges from $400,000 to $600,000 depending on subdivision, age of construction, and lot size. Recent data shows median sale prices in the $390,000 to $450,000 range, with newer construction in Newbury and the surrounding development corridors commanding higher prices closer to $600K and above.

How are the schools in South Fayette Township?

South Fayette Township is served by the South Fayette Township School District, one of the highest-ranked public school systems in Pennsylvania. The district holds an A+ rating from Niche and ranks in the top 5% of Pennsylvania school districts based on standardized test proficiency. Math proficiency averages 74% versus the 38% Pennsylvania average, and reading proficiency averages 87%.

How far is South Fayette Township from downtown Pittsburgh?

South Fayette Township is approximately 17 miles southwest of downtown Pittsburgh. The typical commute runs 15 to 25 minutes via I-79 North to I-376 East, depending on traffic. The township is also 15 minutes from Pittsburgh International Airport, making it a strong choice for travelers and airport-area professionals.

What is South Fayette Township known for?

South Fayette Township is known for its top-ranked school district, the Newbury Market mixed-use development, and being one of the fastest-growing residential corridors in the Pittsburgh region. The township is home to UPMC Children's South, Topgolf, and a steady pipeline of new-construction subdivisions tied to the Newbury growth plan.

What is Newbury Market?

Newbury Market is a large mixed-use brownfield redevelopment in South Fayette Township combining residential, retail, dining, and entertainment uses. A 99,000-square-foot Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World is scheduled to open in 2026 as the first in western Pennsylvania. Other planned components include the Wembley Club pickleball and fitness facility, the Lafayette 180 shopping center, and Apex Newbury residential. Builders invested $75 million in South Fayette construction in 2024 alone.

Is South Fayette Township a good place to raise a family?

Yes. South Fayette Township consistently ranks among the strongest family markets in the Pittsburgh region. The South Fayette Township School District is the primary draw, with top-tier academic outcomes and active extracurricular programs. The township also offers low crime, expansive parks, modern subdivisions, and quick access to UPMC Children's South for pediatric care.

Is the South Fayette Township real estate market appreciating?

Yes. South Fayette Township real estate has appreciated significantly over the past several years, with reported annual appreciation in the high single digits to low double digits at various points. School district demand, the Newbury Market commercial expansion, and continued migration of families seeking top-rated public schools and modern construction have kept the market competitive year over year.

What neighborhoods are near South Fayette Township?

South Fayette Township is bordered by Upper St. Clair, Bridgeville, McDonald, Cecil Township, and North Fayette Township. The broader area includes Collier Township, Mt. Lebanon, and the Robinson Town Center retail corridor. The township sits at the convergence of three school districts — South Fayette, Chartiers Valley, and Canon-McMillan — so confirming the school assignment for any specific address is essential.
