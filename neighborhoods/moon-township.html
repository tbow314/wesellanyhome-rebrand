Moon Township Overview

Moon Township is a township in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, with a population of approximately 25,000 residents. Located 12 miles northwest of downtown Pittsburgh and just 10 to 15 minutes from Pittsburgh International Airport, Moon Township sits at the heart of the airport-area corporate corridor. The township combines established residential neighborhoods, college-town energy from Robert Morris University, and a deep corporate base anchored by FedEx Ground, Nova Chemicals, and the Cherrington Parkway office park.

The corporate footprint is the foundation of the local economy. FedEx Ground is headquartered at 1000 FedEx Drive in Moon Township and employs thousands across the region. Nova Chemicals operates its U.S. Commercial Center on Coraopolis Heights Road. GlaxoSmithKline, Eaton Electrical, Chevron USA, and Coventry Health Care all maintain significant offices in or adjacent to the township. The Cherrington Parkway corporate park alone hosts dozens of additional regional and national companies serving the airport corridor. That stable employer base supports steady housing demand, particularly from professionals who want to live within minutes of the office.

Robert Morris University adds a college-town dimension that few comparable suburbs offer. RMU enrolls thousands of undergraduate and graduate students across more than 60 undergraduate and 20 graduate programs, drawing faculty, staff, and students into the local rental and ownership markets. Combined with the airport access, corporate base, and Moon Area School District, the township offers a balanced market that has held up better than many during regional cycles.

Neighborhoods & Surrounding Areas

Cherrington & Coraopolis Heights

The Cherrington Parkway and Coraopolis Heights area is the corporate heart of Moon Township. Cherrington Corporate Center hosts Eaton, dozens of regional and national company offices, and a growing mix of restaurants and hotels serving the airport corridor. Residential neighborhoods around Cherrington blend established subdivisions with newer construction, and homes here often command the township's higher price points due to proximity to the corporate base.

Buyers drawn to this area are typically professionals working at FedEx Ground, Nova Chemicals, or one of the surrounding corporate offices who want a five-minute commute and walkable access to dining and services.

Robin Hill & University Boulevard

The Robin Hill area and the University Boulevard corridor sit near Robert Morris University and offer a mix of established subdivisions, university-influenced rental properties, and family-oriented neighborhoods. Pricing here typically runs in the high $300Ks to mid $400Ks, with strong inventory turnover from a combination of family relocations, RMU faculty and staff, and corporate transferees.

Robin Hill Park anchors the recreational footprint with sports fields, walking trails, and event space.

Thorn Run & Established Moon

The Thorn Run area and the older established sections of Moon Township offer some of the most accessible price points in the township, often in the high $200Ks to low $300Ks. Housing here ranges from ranches and Cape Cods from the 1960s and 1970s to mid-century split-levels. These neighborhoods appeal to first-time buyers and downsizers who want Moon Area School District access at a more accessible entry price.

Surrounding Airport Communities

Moon Township is bordered by Coraopolis, Crescent Township, Findlay Township, and Robinson Township. The broader airport corridor includes Sewickley and Edgeworth to the north, North Fayette to the south, and Imperial to the west. Each carries its own school district, price profile, and character — but all share the I-376 commuting band and proximity to Pittsburgh International Airport.

Moon Area School District

District Overview

The Moon Area School District serves Moon Township and surrounding airport-area communities. The district benefits from a strong commercial tax base anchored by FedEx Ground, Nova Chemicals, and the Cherrington Parkway corporate corridor, supporting modern facilities, technology integration, and competitive extracurricular programs.

Moon Area offers comprehensive academics, AP and honors course tracks, competitive WPIAL athletics, and career-technical pathways. The district is consistently rated as a solid choice in the Pittsburgh metro and pulls demand from professionals and corporate transferees moving into the airport corridor.

Key Schools

  • Moon Area High School — Grades 9-12. Home of the Tigers. AP and honors offerings, competitive WPIAL athletics, and solid college placement rates.
  • Moon Area Middle School — Grades 6-8. Strong academic foundation feeding into the high school with active extracurriculars.
  • Moon Area Upper Elementary — Upper-elementary grades with modern facility and STEM programming.
  • Moon Area Lower Elementary — Foundation grades serving the township's diverse student population.

Lifestyle & Things to Do

Robert Morris University

Robert Morris University is the township's college anchor. The private university offers more than 60 undergraduate and 20 graduate degree programs, NCAA Division I athletics, and a steady flow of community events, cultural programming, and lectures open to residents. The campus footprint adds a college-town energy that very few suburban Pittsburgh markets can match.

Cherrington Corporate Corridor

Cherrington Parkway and the surrounding corporate park concentrate dozens of national and regional employers, hotels, and a deep restaurant base serving the airport corridor. Residents have walkable or short-drive access to a full range of dining options, fitness facilities, and professional services rare in suburban Pittsburgh markets of comparable price.

Parks & Recreation

Robin Hill Park anchors the township's recreational footprint with sports fields, walking trails, and the historic Robin Hill mansion. Moon Park hosts youth athletics and community events. The Montour Trail passes through the broader airport area for biking and walking, and Sewickley Heights Park offers hiking just to the north. Several private golf clubs sit within a short drive.

Pittsburgh International Airport

Pittsburgh International Airport is 10 to 15 minutes from most of Moon Township, making it one of the most travel-friendly markets in the region. The airport serves more than 50 nonstop destinations and is a major economic engine for the airport corridor. For traveling professionals, frequent flyers, and airline employees, Moon Township is one of the most convenient places to live in western Pennsylvania.

Commute & Transportation

Pittsburgh International Airport

10-15 minutes via I-376 West. One of the most convenient airport-area submarkets in the entire region — ideal for travelers, airline professionals, and corporate transferees.

Downtown Pittsburgh

20-25 minutes via I-376 East. Direct interstate access with predictable drive times outside of major weather events. Reverse commuters from Pittsburgh enjoy a smooth drive against rush-hour traffic.

Cherrington Corporate Center

5-10 minutes from most Moon Township neighborhoods. Many residents work at FedEx Ground, Nova Chemicals, Eaton, GlaxoSmithKline, or one of the dozens of additional Cherrington corridor companies.

I-376 is the primary transportation artery, running east-west through the airport corridor and connecting Moon Township directly to Pittsburgh, the airport, and the Parkway West employer base. University Boulevard and Beaver Grade Road provide secondary corridors with retail and residential access. Public transit options are limited — most residents rely on personal vehicles for daily commuting.

Buying Guide for Moon Township

Buying in Moon Township rewards alignment between your priorities and the right neighborhood. The township spans a wide price band — from older established homes in the high $200Ks near Thorn Run and Moon Run to newer construction in the $450K-$525K range near Cherrington and Coraopolis Heights. Pre-approval is essential, particularly for the more competitive listings near the corporate corridor where airport-area professionals drive demand. Confirm the school assignment for any specific address with the Moon Area School District directly — the 15108 ZIP code overlaps with parts of Coraopolis and surrounding municipalities that fall outside the district. Newer construction near Cherrington commands a premium per square foot but delivers modern floor plans and energy efficiency. Older homes in established neighborhoods offer character and value but expect to budget for HVAC, roofing, or kitchen updates. Compare lot size, basement condition, finished square footage, and proximity to I-376 access. With steady appreciation, a deep corporate tax base, and continued airport-corridor employment, buying in Moon Township positions you in one of the most resilient submarkets in western Pennsylvania.

Selling in Moon Township means leading with the airport corridor employment story and the Moon Area School District. Quality listings priced correctly in Moon Township typically receive offers within the first three weeks, and the corporate base keeps the market resilient even when other regional submarkets soften.
