Why Washington County?

Affordable Compared to Allegheny

Washington County offers significantly lower price-per-square-foot than neighboring Allegheny County while delivering the same quality of life. Buyers get more home for their money — larger lots, newer construction, and lower property taxes. The median home price sits well below the Pittsburgh metro average, making it ideal for first-time buyers and growing families.

Growing Market

Steady 2.5% annual appreciation reflects healthy demand without the volatility of overheated markets. New developments in Cecil Township, Peters Township, and along the I-79 corridor signal continued growth. Population has remained stable as younger professionals discover the area's value proposition.

Southpointe Business Corridor

The Southpointe business park — anchored by companies like CONSOL Energy, Range Resources, and Ansys — brings high-paying jobs directly to Washington County. Employees live minutes from work, eliminating the Pittsburgh commute entirely. Southpointe's continued expansion drives demand for nearby housing in Canonsburg, Cecil, and Peters Township.

Strong Commute to Pittsburgh

I-79 connects Washington County to downtown Pittsburgh in 30-40 minutes. The Southern Beltway (PA 576) further improved access to Pittsburgh International Airport and the western suburbs. Residents enjoy suburban living with straightforward highway access to the city's employment centers, hospitals, and entertainment.
