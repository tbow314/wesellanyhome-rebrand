Living in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania

Allegheny County is the economic and cultural center of Western Pennsylvania, anchored by the city of Pittsburgh and surrounded by 130 municipalities that range from first-ring urban boroughs to affluent, wooded suburbs. With a population of approximately 1.3 million, it is the second-most-populous county in Pennsylvania.

The county offers remarkable diversity in housing stock: century-old Victorian homes in Shadyside and Squirrel Hill, mid-century colonials in Mt. Lebanon and Bethel Park, new-construction luxury in Upper St. Clair and Fox Chapel, and affordable starter homes in Penn Hills, Monroeville, and the South Hills. Median prices across the county range from $165,000 to $425,000, with premium neighborhoods pushing well above $600,000.

Allegheny County is home to some of the best public school districts in Pennsylvania, including Mt. Lebanon, Upper St. Clair, Fox Chapel Area, and North Allegheny. These districts consistently rank among the top in the state and are a major driver of home values in their respective communities.

Pittsburgh's economy has transformed from its steel-era roots into a technology, healthcare, and higher-education hub. Major employers include UPMC, Highmark, Carnegie Mellon University, the University of Pittsburgh, Duquesne University, PNC Financial, and a growing ecosystem of robotics and AI startups. This economic diversity provides stability for the housing market and makes Allegheny County one of the most resilient real estate markets in the northeastern United States.
