Peters Township Overview

Peters Township is an affluent suburb in Washington County, Pennsylvania, with a population of approximately 23,000 residents. Located 20 to 25 minutes south of Pittsburgh along the I-79 corridor, the township is anchored by the McMurray and Venetia communities and is widely recognized as the premier family suburb of southern Pittsburgh. The combination of the Peters Township School District, executive housing stock, organized youth programs, and proximity to both downtown Pittsburgh and the Southpointe business district has made Peters Township one of the most consistently competitive real estate markets in western Pennsylvania.

Buyers move to Peters Township for the schools first and the community second. Peters Township High School is regularly ranked among the top 10 public high schools in Pennsylvania, and the district has held that position year after year. That academic strength shows up in property values: homes inside the district line carry a measurable premium over equivalent homes one township over. Combined with low crime rates, well-maintained neighborhoods, and a deep network of professional and physician households commuting to UPMC, Allegheny Health Network, and Southpointe corporate offices, Peters Township sustains demand even when the broader market softens.

The township is geographically diverse for a suburb. The eastern half along Route 19 holds the densest commercial corridor and the highest concentration of newer construction. Western and southern Peters Township remain more rural, with larger lots, mature trees, and properties that occasionally include creek frontage or two-acre minimums. This range gives buyers genuine options inside one school district, which is rare for a market this competitive.

Market Snapshot

Peters Township's real estate market is one of the most competitive in western Pennsylvania. Inventory is chronically tight, well-priced homes inside the school district frequently receive multiple offers, and average days on market regularly fall into the single digits during the spring and summer selling seasons. Buyers should expect to compete and to come prepared with strong financing or cash positions on premium homes.

Neighborhoods & Surrounding Areas

McMurray

McMurray is the commercial and historic core of Peters Township. Centered along Washington Road (Route 19) and East McMurray Road, the area holds the township's main retail corridor, restaurants, the Peters Township Public Library, and the Donaldson's Crossroads shopping district. Housing in McMurray ranges from established mid-century homes on quarter-acre lots to newer subdivisions tucked behind the commercial corridor.

McMurray carries the township's 15317 ZIP code and is the address most often associated with the Peters Township brand. Buyers seeking convenience and walkability gravitate here.

Venetia

Venetia sits in the southern and eastern portions of the township and is the address of choice for buyers wanting larger lots, more privacy, and an established executive feel. Many homes in Venetia sit on half-acre to two-acre parcels with mature trees and longer driveways. The area carries a 15367 ZIP designation in some pockets and includes some of the township's most expensive homes.

Venetia attracts physicians, attorneys, and corporate executives who value space and discretion while staying inside the Peters Township School District.

Hidden Creek, Brookwood & Newer Subdivisions

The newer planned subdivisions on the eastern side of the township (Hidden Creek, Brookwood, Brush Run, Lincoln Estates) offer modern 4-5 bedroom homes typically built between 1995 and the present. Floor plans favor open-concept living, finished basements, three-car garages, and primary suites. Pricing in these subdivisions starts around $550K and runs through the high $800Ks.

Donaldson's Crossroads & Route 19 Corridor

The Donaldson's Crossroads commercial node serves as the township's hub for shopping, dining, and services. Surrounding residential streets offer a mix of established homes and townhouse communities. Townhouses in Peters Township are limited but command strong prices given the school district premium and proximity to the corridor.

Peters Township School District

District Overview

The Peters Township School District serves approximately 3,972 students across five schools: three elementary schools, one middle school, and Peters Township High School. The district is consistently ranked among the top 10 districts in Pennsylvania and has been listed in the top 5 by Pittsburgh Business Times. U.S. News, Niche, and SchoolDigger all place Peters Township High School inside the top 20 in the state.

The district is the single biggest driver of housing demand in the township. Buyers relocating from out of state often filter their search to the Peters Township boundary first, then evaluate homes within it. Strong AP and dual-enrollment offerings, competitive WPIAL athletics, and high college placement rates support that demand year after year.

Key Schools

  • Peters Township High School — Grades 9-12. Top 10 in Pennsylvania per U.S. News. Wide AP catalog, dual enrollment, competitive WPIAL athletics. The Indians compete at the highest levels in the region.
  • Peters Township Middle School — Grades 6-8. Known for academic rigor, music programs, and strong transition support into the high school.
  • McMurray Elementary — One of three elementary schools serving the district, located near the McMurray commercial core.
  • Pleasant Valley Elementary — Serves the eastern portion of the township including newer subdivisions.
  • Bower Hill Elementary — Serves the northern and western residential pockets of the township.

Lifestyle & Things to Do

Peters Township Community Recreation Center

The Peters Township Community Recreation Center on East McMurray Road is one of the most well-equipped municipal recreation facilities in the region. The center includes an indoor pool, fitness equipment, group exercise studios, gymnasiums, and event space. Resident memberships are a meaningful amenity and a common talking point for buyers comparing Peters to other suburbs.

Arrowhead Trail & Peterswood Park

The Arrowhead Trail is a paved multi-use trail running through the township, connecting parks, neighborhoods, and the recreation center. Peterswood Park anchors the trail with playgrounds, sports fields, pavilions, and dog-walking areas. The trail is heavily used year-round and is a defining lifestyle amenity for active families.

Dining & Shopping

The Donaldson's Crossroads area and the Route 19 corridor hold the township's restaurants, coffee shops, and retail. Locally owned options include Atria's, Vallozzi's, and a strong rotation of independent cafes. Trader Joe's, Whole Foods (in nearby Bridgeville), and traditional grocery anchors are within 10 minutes. Buyers used to suburban convenience find the mix here mature and reliable.

Country Clubs & Golf

St. Clair Country Club, Valley Brook Country Club, and Southpointe Golf Club are all within a short drive. The country club culture is part of the social fabric of the upper end of the Peters Township market, and many executive housing transactions involve buyers who already hold club memberships in the area.

Commute & Transportation

Downtown Pittsburgh

20-25 minutes via I-79 North to I-279. Direct interstate access through the Bridgeville exit. Off-peak commutes are closer to 20 minutes; peak morning into the city runs 30-35.

Southpointe Business District

10-15 minutes via I-79 South. Energy sector and corporate professionals who work at CONSOL, Ansys, or Range Resources find Peters Township a strong fit, trading slightly longer commutes for the school district.

Pittsburgh International Airport

25-30 minutes via I-79 North to the Parkway West. Convenient for traveling executives. Many physician and corporate households fly weekly without major friction.

I-79 is the primary transportation artery, with the Bridgeville and Canonsburg exits serving the township. Route 19 runs north-south through McMurray and provides commercial access. Public transit options are limited and most residents rely on personal vehicles. UPMC South Hills, AHN, and Southpointe corporate offices are all within 15 minutes.

Who Should Buy in Peters Township

Families Prioritizing Schools

Executives & Physicians

Relocations into Pittsburgh

Out-of-state buyers relocating into Pittsburgh for healthcare, energy, or technology roles consistently land in Peters Township. The combination of schools, housing stock, and proximity to both downtown and Southpointe makes it the easiest answer to the relocation question.

Buying Guide for Peters Township

Buying in Peters Township requires preparation and speed. Average days on market frequently fall into the single digits during peak season, and well-priced homes inside the school district often receive multiple offers within 48 hours. Buyers should be fully pre-approved (or cash-ready), have inspection contingencies tightened, and be willing to write strong offers without long negotiation cycles. Decide upfront whether you want established Venetia (larger lots, mature trees, older homes), McMurray convenience (closer to retail and schools), or newer construction in Hidden Creek, Brookwood, or Brush Run (modern floor plans, higher prices, lower maintenance). Confirm school assignment within the township for elementary boundaries, since not every street in the 15317 ZIP code feeds the same elementary school. Compare properties on lot size, basement finish, garage capacity, and proximity to the recreation center and Arrowhead Trail. The 3.6% annual appreciation trend signals a market with sustained demand. Buying now positions you ahead of continued school-driven demand and the broader strength of the Pittsburgh executive housing market.

Frequently Asked Questions About Peters Township Real Estate

What is the average home price in Peters Township, PA?

The median home price in Peters Township ranges from $500,000 to $800,000, depending on neighborhood, lot size, and finish level. Newer construction in communities like Hidden Creek and Brookwood and larger executive homes in Venetia anchor the upper end, while established homes in older McMurray subdivisions offer entry points closer to the $500K mark.

How are the schools in Peters Township?

Peters Township is served by the Peters Township School District, which serves approximately 3,972 students across five schools. Peters Township High School is consistently ranked among the top 10 high schools in Pennsylvania by U.S. News and Niche, with strong AP offerings, college placement rates, and athletic programs. The district is the single biggest driver of demand in this market.

How far is Peters Township from downtown Pittsburgh?

Peters Township is approximately 20 to 25 minutes from downtown Pittsburgh via I-79 North to I-279. Most residents access Pittsburgh through the Bridgeville exit or the Route 19 corridor. Reverse commuters to Southpointe or Canonsburg find the drive to be 10 to 15 minutes.

What is Peters Township known for?

Peters Township is known for the Peters Township School District (one of the top-ranked districts in Pennsylvania), the McMurray and Venetia communities, the Peters Township Community Recreation Center, the Arrowhead Trail, and a deep concentration of executive, professional, and physician households. It is widely considered the premier family-focused suburb of southern Pittsburgh.

Is Peters Township a good place to raise a family?

Yes. Peters Township is consistently ranked among the best places to raise a family in western Pennsylvania. The school district, low crime rates, organized youth sports programs, the Peters Township Community Recreation Center, and proximity to Mingo Creek and Peterswood Park make it a destination for families relocating into the Pittsburgh region.

What are the major neighborhoods in Peters Township?

Major neighborhoods include McMurray (the commercial and historic core), Venetia (older established homes with larger lots), Hidden Creek, Brookwood, Brush Run, Lincoln Estates, and the Donaldson's Crossroads area. Newer construction in the eastern half of the township off Route 19 commands the highest premiums.

Is the Peters Township real estate market competitive?

Yes. Peters Township is one of the most competitive real estate markets in the Pittsburgh region. Well-priced homes in the school district often receive multiple offers within days, with average days on market frequently in the single digits. Annual appreciation has tracked approximately 3.6%, supported by school demand and limited inventory.

What neighborhoods are near Peters Township?

Peters Township borders Bethel Park to the north, Upper St. Clair to the northwest, Cecil Township and Canonsburg to the west, and North Strabane Township to the south. Buyers comparing options often look at Upper St. Clair and Mt. Lebanon for similar school quality at higher price points, or Canonsburg for lower entry pricing.
