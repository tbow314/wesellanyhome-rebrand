## 2. What GTM Means & the Ticker Change

### GTM as a Business Concept

**Go-To-Market (GTM) strategy** is the plan a company uses to bring a product or service to market and reach target customers. It encompasses:

- **Market research** -- understanding target market, customer needs, and trends
- **Product positioning** -- defining your USP and how you solve customer problems
- **Pricing strategy** -- based on costs, demand, competition, and perceived value
- **Distribution channels** -- selecting the most effective routes to reach customers
- **Sales & marketing plan** -- the tactical playbook for demand gen and closing

**GTM strategy vs. marketing strategy:** Marketing is the broader, long-term brand and market play. GTM zooms in on specific product launches or new market entries.

**B2B GTM** tends to be longer and more complex due to multiple stakeholders, longer sales cycles, and formal purchasing processes.

### ZoomInfo's Ticker Change to $GTM

**Date:** May 12, 2025 -- ZoomInfo changed its Nasdaq symbol from **ZI** to **GTM**.

**What it signals:**
- A full strategic pivot from "data provider" to **end-to-end GTM intelligence platform**
- CEO Henry Schuck: *"Changing our ticker to GTM isn't just symbolic -- it reflects our belief in a future where go-to-market teams aren't stuck between ideas and execution."*
- A public company changing its ticker takes months of planning, legal work, and investor comms -- this is a hard commitment to a new identity
- Validates GTM as a **permanent fixture** in the modern business strategy playbook
- New roles emerging: Head of GTM Strategy, GTM Architect, GTM Operator

**Market reaction:** Shares jumped 7.4% to $10.04 on announcement day.

**Simultaneous launch:** GTM Studio launched the same day, plus the GTM 25 Roadshow (NYC, Boston, SF, London).

## 4. GARR (Gross ARR Retention) & the 85% to 105% Motion

### What is GARR / GRR?

**Gross ARR Retention (GARR)** -- also called Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) or Gross Dollar Retention (GDR) -- measures the percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, **excluding** any upsells, cross-sells, or expansion.

**Formula:**
```
GARR = (Starting ARR - Churn ARR - Contraction ARR) / Starting ARR x 100%
```

**Key characteristics:**
- GARR is always < 100% (it excludes expansion)
- It reveals the true health of your base -- churn can't hide behind upsell success
- Calculated on a cohort basis (track specific customers over a 12-month period)

### Benchmarks

| Segment | Typical GRR |
|---------|-------------|
| Enterprise SaaS | 90-97% |
| Mid-Market | 85-92% |
| SMB | 75-85% |
| Industry Median | 89% |
| Top Quartile | 95%+ |

### The 85% Renewal to 105% NRR Motion for a Mid-Market AM

Here's how the math works:

**Starting point:** You manage a $1M book of renewals

| Step | What Happens | Revenue Impact |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Gross Retention (85%)** | You retain 85% of your book -- meaning 15% churns or downgrades | $850K retained |
| **Upsell/Expansion** | You upsell retained accounts (additional seats, higher tiers, new products) | +$200K expansion |
| **Net Revenue Retention (105%)** | $850K + $200K = $1.05M from same cohort | 105% NRR |

**What this means practically:**
- Even with 15% churn/downgrade, strong expansion can yield **net growth** from existing customers
- 105% NRR means the company grows revenue **without acquiring any new customers**
- This is a strong signal of product-market fit and effective AM motion

**Why 85% GRR is the floor:**
- Below 85% = "leaky bucket" -- your churn is so high that expansion can't sustainably cover it
- Below 85% = red flag for investors, signals product quality or service issues
- A $50M ARR company at 85% GRR is losing $7.5M/year -- "treadmill growth"

**The AM's role in this motion:**
- **Protect the base** -- drive renewals, manage risk, keep GRR above 85%
- **Expand the base** -- identify upsell opportunities within existing accounts
- **Dedicated AM impact:** Customers with dedicated AMs show 94% GRR vs. 78% for self-serve
- Companies with NRR > 100% or GRR > 85% grow **1.5-3x faster**

**For investors:** Break down GRR by segment (SMB, mid-market, enterprise) to show where you're strongest.

## 5. Best Interview Questions for a Mid-Market AM Renewals Role

### Questions About Team Performance & Quota

1. **"How many AMs on the team are hitting quota today? What's the YoY trend?"**
   - Shows you care about being set up for success, and reveals if quotas are realistic

2. **"What does the current book structure look like -- how many accounts per AM, what's the average ARR per account?"**
   - Helps you assess workload and whether the motion is manageable

3. **"What's the team's current GRR and NRR? Where do you want those numbers to be in 12 months?"**
   - Demonstrates you understand retention metrics and think about outcomes

4. **"What does ramping look like for a new AM? When am I expected to be fully productive?"**
   - Practical question that shows you think about execution

### Questions About the Renewals Motion

5. **"At what point in the renewal cycle do AMs engage? 90 days out? 120?"**
   - Shows you understand renewal process mechanics

6. **"What's the biggest driver of churn or downgrades today, and what's being done about it?"**
   - Shows strategic thinking about protecting the base

7. **"How do you handle expansion/upsell -- is it the AM's responsibility or is there a separate team?"**
   - Clarifies scope and comp structure

8. **"What does the handoff from new business AE to AM look like? What's the typical onboarding experience for a new customer?"**
   - Reveals potential friction points that affect renewals

### Questions About Tools, Support & Culture

9. **"What tools does the team use today? (CRM, ZoomInfo, Gong, etc.) What tools do you wish you had?"**
   - Shows tech-savviness and reveals investment in the team

10. **"What do our 1-on-1s look like? How do you coach and develop AMs?"**
    - Reveals leadership style and commitment to your growth

11. **"What type of AM is most successful here? What do your top performers do differently?"**
    - Gives you a blueprint for success and reveals culture

### Questions About Company Direction

12. **"What's the company's growth strategy beyond revenue -- are you heading toward IPO, raising, expanding into new markets?"**
    - Gives insight into stability and trajectory

13. **"What made you take this leadership role? What excites you most about the team right now?"**
    - Reveals what leadership values and whether there's genuine enthusiasm

14. **"How does the AM team collaborate cross-functionally with CS, Product, and Marketing?"**
    - Shows you understand AMs don't operate in a silo

### The Power Move Question

15. **"Why do you do it that way?"**
    - Apply this to any process they describe. If the leader is thoughtful, they'll appreciate the challenge. It positions you as a strategic thinker, not just an order-taker.

### Pro Tips for the Interview
- Use the **SOAR Method** (Situation, Obstacle, Action, Result) for behavioral answers
- Quantify everything: "I retained 92% of a $3.2M book and drove 108% NRR"
- Reference ZoomInfo's own metrics and products -- shows you did your homework
- Frame questions around **being set up for success** -- good managers love this
